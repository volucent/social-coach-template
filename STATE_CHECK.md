# State Check

Use before replying to a heated message, joining a charged meeting, escalating, or making
a social decision that feels unusually expensive.

## 30-Second Check

Answer fast:

- Am I trying to solve the problem or win the trial?
- Is this urgent, or does my nervous system think it is urgent?
- Would I be comfortable with this message being read by a neutral observer tomorrow?
- Am I replying to the actual request or to the pattern behind it?
- What outcome do I want after this thread is over?
- What is the smallest boring move that preserves credibility and safety?

## Body Cues That Mean Do Not Send Yet

- Heat in face or chest.
- Fast typing.
- Urge to include old history.
- Urge to make them admit the pattern.
- Sarcasm feels irresistible.
- The message is being edited for maximum impact instead of minimum safe clarity.

If two or more are true, do not send yet.

## Traffic Light

Green:

- Calm enough to be concise.
- Can state the request without contempt.
- Can accept that the other person may not admit the pattern today.

Best move: reply normally.

Yellow:

- Agitated but still able to choose.
- Want to be sharp.
- Need the issue handled, but it is not a true emergency.

Best move: delay, ask for written specifics, or send a boring process reply.

Red:

- Want to punish, expose, or corner someone.
- Cannot stop replaying the pattern.
- Body feels trapped or flooded.
- The message feels satisfying because it lands hard.

Best move: no substantive reply. Use a delay script and regulate.

## Delay Scripts

> I need a little time to review this and avoid giving a rushed answer. I will follow up.

> I need to check the facts before answering. I will respond once I have them in front of me.

> I do not want to answer from memory here. Please put the expected behavior and deadline in writing, and I will review.

## Sendability Test

A message is probably safe if it is:

- Short.
- Procedural.
- Not sarcastic.
- Not diagnosing anyone.
- Clear about what is needed next.
- Written for the audience, not for emotional release.

If it needs emotional release, put that in a private debrief, not the thread.
