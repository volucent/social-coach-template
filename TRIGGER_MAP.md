# Trigger Map

This file maps recurring activation patterns. Use it to choose the right move, not to
prove that the feeling is wrong.

Keep this file generic. Put personal examples in `private/`.

## Trapped

Feels like:

- No exit.
- Forced endurance.
- Growing agitation or panic.
- Urge to flee, snap, or disappear.

Common situations:

- Long meetings with no agenda.
- Family obligations.
- Large group events where leaving feels rude.
- Conversations with no clear endpoint.

Threat-story:

- "I am stuck and nobody cares."

Usually more accurate:

- The situation may be unpleasant or low-agency, but an exit can often be installed.

Best move:

- Add a hard stop.
- Sit near an exit.
- Take a reset break.
- Define the job of being there.
- Leave before red state.

## Re-Explaining The Same Thing

Feels like:

- Contempt.
- Despair.
- Urge to say "I already explained this."

Common situations:

- Old decisions resurfacing.
- People using outdated terminology.
- Repeated questions with no durable source of truth.

Threat-story:

- "They do not respect my work or attention."

Usually more accurate:

- The source of truth is not durable enough, or people are not required to use it.

Best move:

- Ask for current expected behavior.
- Link or update the source of truth.
- Stop being the memory system.

Script:

> I want to avoid reconstructing this from memory. Please link the current doc or write the expected behavior here so we can update the source of truth.

## Ambiguous Authority

Feels like:

- Defiance.
- Anger at being ordered around without clarity.
- Need to prove the authority is wrong.

Common situations:

- Unclear decision rights.
- Verbal decisions with downstream risk.
- Accountability landing on someone who did not make the decision.

Threat-story:

- "An unsafe authority is controlling me and making me responsible."

Usually more accurate:

- Decision rights are unclear and need a written operating model.

Best move:

- Ask for owner, approval, acceptance criteria, and risk context.
- Move decisions into writing.
- Escalate process, not personality.

## Unfair Blame

Feels like:

- Instant prosecution mode.
- Need to show receipts.
- Fear of being seen as incompetent or bad.

Common situations:

- Someone implies you missed something.
- A vague request becomes urgent.
- A system gap is personalized.

Threat-story:

- "If I do not prove the whole history now, I will be blamed."

Usually more accurate:

- One clear factual reply often protects better than a full trial.

Best move:

- State current facts.
- State blocker.
- State next required input.
- Save receipts privately.

## Low-Signal Social Ritual

Feels like:

- Dread.
- Boredom.
- Guilt for not wanting what other people want.

Common situations:

- Large groups.
- Loud venues.
- Repetitive rituals.
- Events where the activity prevents real connection.

Threat-story:

- "I am bad at friendship if I do not go."

Usually more accurate:

- The format may be wrong even if the person matters.

Best move:

- Use `CODEX.md`.
- Decline the format.
- Counter-offer a better format if the person matters.

## Hidden Expectations

Feels like:

- Irritation and distrust.
- Sense that the game is rigged.
- Pressure to infer unspoken rules.

Common situations:

- Meetings with no agenda.
- Last-minute deadlines.
- Social expectations nobody stated directly.

Threat-story:

- "I am being set up to fail."

Usually more accurate:

- Expectations are implicit and need to be made explicit or declined.

Best move:

- Ask what decision is needed.
- Ask for deadline and owner.
- Decline if there is no actual decision or obligation.
