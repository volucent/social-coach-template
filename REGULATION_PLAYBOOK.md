# Regulation Playbook

Use this when emotion is high enough that the next reply might become expensive.

## Prime Rule

Do not optimize the argument while flooded. First lower arousal, then choose the move.

The goal is not to become calm forever. The goal is to get enough control back that the
next action is chosen deliberately instead of from threat state.

## Green State

Signs:

- Can be brief.
- Can see more than one interpretation.
- Can ask for what is needed without contempt.
- Not trying to make anyone admit the whole pattern right now.

Allowed actions:

- Send a concise reply.
- Ask for specifics.
- Make a decision.

## Yellow State

Signs:

- Agitated.
- Can still pause if prompted.

Protocol:

1. Stop for 90 seconds.
2. Take one to three slow exhales.
3. Put feet flat and press into the floor.
4. Look around and name three real objects.
5. Decide whether the next move is delay, process reply, boundary, or no reply.

Yellow-state rule:

> If the reply can wait 10 minutes, it waits 10 minutes.

## Red State

Signs:

- Urge to expose, punish, corner, or finally prove the whole thing.
- Heart rate elavated.
- Urge to lash out.
- The words feel satisfying.

Protocol:

1. Do not act.
2. Stand up if possible.
3. Leave the room, step into a hallway, or get outside.
4. Take three slow exhales.
5. Revisit after at least 20 minutes.

Red-state rule:

> If it feels like justice, it is probably not ready to send.
