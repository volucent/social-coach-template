# Privacy Checklist

Use this before making the repository public, pushing a branch, creating a fork, or asking
an AI assistant to summarize the repository.

## Never Commit

- Real names, aliases, addresses, phone numbers, email addresses, handles, or photos.
- Employer names, customer names, project names, account details, ticket links, or Slack links.
- Medical history, diagnoses, medication, trauma history, or family history.
- Screenshots, transcripts, copied messages, or private quotes.
- Live conflict notes, dates, timelines, or evidence logs involving real people.
- Secrets, API keys, credentials, tokens, or private URLs.

## Safe To Commit

- Generic rules and scripts.
- Redacted templates with bracket placeholders.
- Fictional examples that cannot be mapped to real people.
- Prompt skills that refer to "the user" rather than a named person.
- Public documentation about how to use the template.

## Before Publishing

- Confirm `private/` is ignored by git.
- Search for your name, your employer, your city, and names of people close to you.
- Search for known project names, aliases, and conflict-specific phrases.
- Remove `.DS_Store`, screenshots, exports, and archives.
- Do not copy `.git/` history from a private source repository.
- If anything feels borderline, move it to `private/` or replace it with a placeholder.

## Recommended Search Terms

Run searches for:

- Your legal name and common nicknames.
- Friends, family, coworkers, and partners.
- Employer, clients, products, projects, and internal systems.
- Health, trauma, therapy, diagnosis, medication, or crisis terms.
- City, neighborhood, phone, email, usernames, and handles.
